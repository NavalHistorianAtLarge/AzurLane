  • 把 is used to bring the object before the verb, a situation that would otherwise be grammatically incorrect in Chinese.
  • 片 means disk, part, but is also a classifier for large areas (such as a tract of land or an area of the ocean), or for small flat things (disks, cds, etc). Here it would make sense if this character meant "entire" (as in "this entire sea area").
  • 住 is used to indicate that something is firm or stable.
  • 的: Modifies 惊人
  • を: Marks direct object.
  • ね: Yomichan lists the definition used above under its third etymology as a particle.

Apparently, the Sanctuary has been around for a long time, as was already indicated by the fact that legends may have grown up around it (VDiR1:22). But who would have come to seek it earlier? Humans? Shipgirls? Sirens? Animals? Were there any such beings regularly going missing from the Sakura Empire, like a Holy Grail search or something?

The EN line is dubious, suggesting that Zui might have been seeing wreckage around her, rather than just surmising on the fact that others were probably less fortunate. She doesn't outright indicate that there were other comers.
